Shoulder Mobility: Flexion & Extension

Improve your shoulder mobility with these guided progressions. Focus on flexion and extension to enhance your range of motion and prevent injury.

  • Flexion
  • Extension
  • Tips

Shoulder Flexion

Shoulder flexion is essential for movements that involve raising your arms above your head. Follow these progressions for improved flexibility and strength.

Progression 1: Wall Slides

Stand facing away from a wall, with your back and arms against it. Slowly slide your arms up while keeping contact with the wall. Repeat for 10-15 repetitions.

Progression 2: PVC Pipe Pass-throughs

Hold a PVC pipe with a wide grip. Keeping your arms straight, bring the pipe from the front of your body to the back. This helps increase shoulder range and flexibility. Progress this by placing your arms closer together.

Shoulder Extension

Shoulder extension is crucial for any movement that involves bringing your arms behind your body. These progressions aim to improve extension range and strength.

Progression 1: Standing Arm Reaches

Stand upright and reach your arms behind you, interlocking your fingers if possible. Gently lift your arms to feel a stretch in your shoulders and chest.

Progression 2: Table Top Lift Offs

Sit on the floor with your hands placed behind you, fingers pointing away. Push through your hands to lift your hips, focusing on extending your shoulders.

Tips for Success

  • Perform these exercises regularly to see improvements in your shoulder mobility.
  • Focus on proper form and technique to avoid injury and maximize results.
  • Listen to your body and adjust the intensity or range of motion as needed.
  • Consult with a fitness professional or physical therapist for personalized guidance.
