Introduction to Handstands

Handstands are not just a test of balance; they are a comprehensive way to build strength, control, and body awareness. Whether you are a beginner or looking to refine your technique, our guide is here to help.

Benefits of Handstands

  • Improves upper body strength
  • Enhances core stability and control
  • Develops balance and proprioception
  • Increases wrist flexibility and strength

Handstand Progressions

Mastering a handstand is a process. Progress through each stage at your own pace for the best results.

Progression 1: Wrist Warm-Up

Begin each session by preparing your wrists. Perform stretches and mobility exercises to increase flexibility and prevent injuries.

Progression 2: Wall Planks

Start with wall planks to build shoulder strength and get comfortable with bearing your weight on your hands. Gradually increase the angle to move closer to a full handstand position.

Progression 3: Wall Handstand

Progress to a wall handstand. Begin with your back to the wall, slowly walk your feet up while walking your hands closer to the wall. Aim for a straight line from hands to toes.

Progression 4: Freestanding Handstand Practice

Once comfortable with wall handstands, begin practicing freestanding handstands. Use a spotter or practice on a soft surface to reduce the risk of injury.

Handstand Tips

  • Focus on form: Keep your body in a straight line, from your wrists to your toes.
  • Practice regularly: Consistency is key to improvement.
  • Be patient: Progress takes time and practice.
  • Record your practice: Video recordings can help identify areas for improvement.
